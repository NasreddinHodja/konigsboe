So what is $n$? Umm...

For 3, 5, 7 edges we have:

#+id: 17

\begin{align*}
3 = n \times 2 + 1 \quad &\implies \quad n = 1 \\

\end{align*}

Yeah, why stop at 3? *we could go on*! Starting from $n = 1$, to infinity!!!!

What we found out here is that 3 is not special. Neither is 5, or 7. What IS special is this formula:

\begin{gather*}
2n + 1 \\
\text{where }n\text{ starts at }1\text{ and goes up}
\end{gather*}

+ All vertices that have a number of edges that can be written as that formula are bound to behave like [[fig:17][this]]!
